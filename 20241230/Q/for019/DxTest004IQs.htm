Data Acquisition and Imaging Quiz

1. What is the process of creating an exact copy of a storage device called?
Disk Imaging
2. What tool is used to prevent data from being written to a storage device during the acquisition process?
Write Blocker
3. What is the primary purpose of a write blocker in digital forensics?
To prevent altering data during acquisition
4. What is the term for collecting data from a running system without shutting it down?
Live Acquisition
5. What is the process of gathering data from a powered-off system called?
Dead Acquisition
6. What type of data is typically captured using disk imaging during a forensic investigation?
All data, including deleted files and slack space
7. Which of the following is a method used to prevent changes to evidence during digital forensics?
Creating a bit-by-bit copy of the data
8. What is the term for the unused space on a storage medium that may still contain fragments of deleted data?
Slack Space
9. What is the standard method for ensuring that data acquisition is not tampered with during the process?
Hashing
10. What is the primary difference between live and dead data acquisition?
Live acquisition collects data from an active system, while dead acquisition collects data from a powered-off system.

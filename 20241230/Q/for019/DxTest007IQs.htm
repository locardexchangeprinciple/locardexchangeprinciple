Mobile Device Forensics Quiz

1. What type of data can be extracted from mobile devices during forensics investigations?
Text messages, call logs, app data, GPS locations, images, and videos.
2. What is the purpose of a Faraday bag in mobile device forensics?
To prevent remote wiping or tampering with the device during forensic examination.
3. What are the challenges faced when extracting data from encrypted mobile devices?
The need for passwords, PINs, or other forms of authentication to access encrypted data.
4. Which operating systems are primarily encountered in mobile forensics investigations?
iOS and Android
5. What is a mobile device's 'rooting' process in Android forensics?
Rooting is the process of gaining privileged control over the Android operating system to access hidden data.
6. In iOS forensics, what tool is commonly used to extract data from an iPhone?
XRY or Cellebrite
7. What is the purpose of logical extraction in mobile forensics?
To extract user-accessible data such as contacts, call logs, and messages from a mobile device.
8. What is 'JTAG' used for in mobile device forensics?
JTAG is used for direct access to the mobile device's memory for data extraction, even if the device is locked.
9. Which mobile app data is frequently analyzed in mobile forensics investigations?
Social media apps, messaging apps, email apps, and GPS/navigation apps.
10. What is the role of mobile forensics in criminal investigations?
To provide critical evidence such as location data, communications, and digital interactions that can support or contradict a suspect's alibi.

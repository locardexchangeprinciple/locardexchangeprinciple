Introduction to Digital Forensics Quiz

1. What is the primary goal of digital forensics?

  • A. To prevent cybercrime
  • B. To gather and preserve digital evidence
  • C. To create digital data

Correct! The primary goal is to gather and preserve digital evidence.

Incorrect. Prevention is a cybersecurity goal, not digital forensics.

Incorrect. Creating data is not the goal of digital forensics.

2. Which term best describes "the application of computer science techniques to solve legal issues"?

  • A. Digital Evidence
  • B. Digital Forensics
  • C. Cybersecurity

Correct! Digital forensics focuses on solving legal issues using computer science.

Incorrect. Digital evidence is an artifact, not a process.

Incorrect. Cybersecurity deals with protecting systems, not solving legal issues.

3. What is the chain of custody?

  • A. A process to ensure evidence integrity
  • B. A list of suspects in a cybercrime
  • C. The chronological sequence of a cyberattack

Correct! The chain of custody ensures evidence integrity throughout an investigation.

Incorrect. It does not pertain to suspects.

Incorrect. It refers to evidence handling, not attack timelines.

4. What is considered digital evidence?

  • A. A physical object found at the crime scene
  • B. Information stored or transmitted in binary form
  • C. Testimonies of eyewitnesses

Correct! Digital evidence includes any data stored or transmitted in binary form.

Incorrect. Physical objects are not considered digital evidence.

Incorrect. Eyewitness testimonies are not digital evidence.

5. Which of the following is NOT a step in the digital forensic process?

  • A. Collection
  • B. Analysis
  • C. Fabrication

Correct! Fabrication is not a part of the forensic process.

Incorrect. Collection is a key step in digital forensics.

Incorrect. Analysis is a critical phase in digital forensics.

6. What does the term "forensically sound" mean?

  • A. Evidence is collected, analyzed, and presented in a manner that maintains integrity
  • B. Evidence is loud enough to be heard in court
  • C. Evidence is kept in a soundproof environment

Correct! "Forensically sound" means maintaining the integrity of evidence throughout the process.

Incorrect. This is not related to audio levels.

Incorrect. Soundproofing has no relevance to digital evidence.

7. What is the first step in the digital forensic process?

  • A. Preservation
  • B. Analysis
  • C. Collection

Correct! Collection is the initial step in the digital forensic process.

Incorrect. Preservation typically follows collection.

Incorrect. Analysis occurs later in the process.

8. Why is the chain of custody important?

  • A. To ensure evidence is admissible in court
  • B. To create backups of evidence
  • C. To train investigators

Correct! The chain of custody is crucial for ensuring evidence admissibility in court.

Incorrect. Backups are not the primary purpose of the chain of custody.

Incorrect. Training is unrelated to the chain of custody.

9. What does "hash value" refer to in digital forensics?

  • A. A type of encryption algorithm
  • B. A unique identifier for data integrity verification
  • C. A storage unit for evidence

Incorrect. Hash values are not encryption algorithms.

Correct. Hash values uniquely identifies the contents of a file.

Incorrect. Hash values are not storage units.

10. Which of the following tools is commonly used for disk imaging in digital forensics?

  • A. EnCase
  • B. FTK Imager
  • C. Wireshark

Correct! FTK Imager is a popular tool for disk imaging.

Incorrect. EnCase is primarily used for analysis, not specifically for disk imaging.

Incorrect. Wireshark is used for network packet analysis, not disk imaging.
